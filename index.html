Solar and Lunar Eclipses

A solar eclipse occurs when the Moon passes between the Sun and the Earth, blocking the Sun's rays and casting a shadow on the Earth.

A lunar eclipse occurs when the Earth passes between the Sun and the Moon, casting a shadow on the Moon.

How They Happen

A solar eclipse can only occur during a new moon when the Moon passes directly between the Earth and the Sun. This can happen up to 5 times a year, but it's not visible from all parts of the Earth each time.

A lunar eclipse can only occur during a full moon when the Earth is directly between the Sun and the Moon. This can happen up to 3 times a year, and it's visible from anywhere on the night side of the Earth.
